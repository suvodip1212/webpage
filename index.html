About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at ETH Zurich, affiliated with the Future Resilient Systems (FRS) programme at the Singapore-ETH Centre. My expertise lies at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Applied Eye-Tracking.

I bridge the gap between physiological signal processing (developed during my MS at IIT Kharagpur) and system resilience (the focus of my PhD at ETH Zurich). My work aims to create "Cognition-Aware" interfaces that can sense a user's mental state to support critical decision-making in urban and industrial systems.

Research Vision

My research seeks to advance decision-making support tools toward more robust and uncertainty-aware solutions through field experiments, numerical simulation, and HCI.

Cognitive State Estimation

Using minute pupil size differences (Anisocoria) and saccadic signatures to quantify mental workload unobtrusively.

Uncertainty Visualization

Studying how spatial data visualization impacts the cognitive load of operators in time-critical safety domains.

System Resilience

Designing notification frameworks for predicted disruptions in complex cyber-physical urban systems.
